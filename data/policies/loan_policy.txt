LOAN POLICY — RETAIL AND CORPORATE LENDING
Document ID: POL-LOAN-002
Version: 2.7
Effective Date: 01 April 2024
Owner: Credit Risk and Retail Banking Division

1. OBJECTIVE
This policy governs the sanctioning, disbursement, monitoring, and recovery of loans across all segments: retail, MSME, agriculture, and corporate. It is aligned with the RBI Master Circular on Loans and Advances and the Bank's Credit Risk Framework.

2. LOAN CATEGORIES
2.1 Retail Loans:
    - Home Loans (max LTV: 80% for loans up to ₹30 lakh; 75% for ₹30–75 lakh; 70% above ₹75 lakh)
    - Personal Loans (unsecured; max ₹25 lakh; min CIBIL score: 700)
    - Vehicle Loans (up to 85% on-road price for new vehicles)
    - Education Loans (up to ₹75 lakh for overseas education with collateral)

2.2 MSME Loans:
    - Loans up to ₹1 crore: collateral-free under CGTMSE scheme
    - Working capital limit: max 20% of projected annual turnover
    - Priority Sector Lending (PSL) categorization per RBI guidelines

2.3 Corporate Loans:
    - Minimum credit rating: internal rating ≥ BBB or external rating equivalent
    - Board approval required for exposures above ₹500 crore
    - Consortium/Syndication arrangements as per internal policy

3. CREDIT APPRAISAL
3.1 All applications must include:
    - Income proof (last 3 years IT returns for self-employed)
    - Bank statements (minimum 12 months)
    - Credit Bureau Report (CIBIL, CRIF, Equifax, or Experian)
    - Property valuation report (certified valuers only, empaneled list)

3.2 Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio limits:
    - Up to ₹5 lakh annual income: max DTI 40%
    - ₹5–15 lakh annual income: max DTI 50%
    - Above ₹15 lakh annual income: max DTI 55%

4. INTEREST RATES AND CHARGES
4.1 All retail floating-rate loans must be linked to External Benchmark Lending Rate (EBLR), currently the RBI Repo Rate.
4.2 Reset frequency: quarterly (mandatory per RBI circular DBOD.No.Dir.BC.88/13.03.000/2019-20)
4.3 No pre-payment penalty on floating rate loans.
4.4 Processing fee: maximum 1% of loan amount + applicable GST, non-refundable.

5. DISBURSEMENT
5.1 Loan must be disbursed within 7 working days of complete document submission.
5.2 Home loan disbursements must align with construction stage (for under-construction properties).
5.3 Direct disbursement to builder/seller accounts mandatory for property value > ₹20 lakh.

6. NPA CLASSIFICATION AND PROVISIONING
6.1 Sub-standard: Overdue 90+ days
6.2 Doubtful: Remained NPA for 12+ months
6.3 Loss: Identified by bank/auditors/RBI inspection
Provisioning as per RBI IRAC norms; reviewed quarterly.

7. RESTRUCTURING AND RESOLUTION
7.1 One-time restructuring permissible for genuine business distress cases.
7.2 All restructured accounts above ₹25 crore require independent credit audit.
7.3 Refer MSME accounts under RBI Resolution Framework 2.0 where applicable.

8. COLLECTION AND RECOVERY
8.1 Collections must follow RBI Fair Practices Code and BCSBI guidelines.
8.2 Recovery agents must be trained, certified, and on the approved panel.
8.3 Legal action (SARFAESI/DRT) to be initiated for NPAs above ₹1 crore after 6 months of NPA classification.
